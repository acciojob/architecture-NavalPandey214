Lord Hanuman, also known as Hanumanji or Anjaneya, is a prominent deity in Hinduism and a central character in the Indian epic, the Ramayana. He is revered as a divine figure, symbolizing devotion, strength, loyalty, and righteousness.
Background and Origin: Lord Hanuman is believed to be the son of Anjana and Kesari. He is often depicted as a monkey-faced deity due to a divine curse that gave him a monkey-like appearance. Devotion to Lord Rama: Hanuman is most famous for his unwavering devotion to Lord Rama, the protagonist of the Ramayana. He played a pivotal role in Lord Rama's mission to rescue his wife, Sita, from the demon king Ravana. In the Ramayana, he exhibited numerous feats of strength, including jumping across the ocean to reach the island of Lanka. Divine Abilities: Hanuman is also known for his divine powers, which include the ability to change his size at will, fly, and even carry entire mountains in his hands.
